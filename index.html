      The Sainte-Laguë Method: How Seats Are Allocated Proportionately

      Step 1: Divide (Calculate Quotients)

      • Start with the total votes received by each party.
      • Divide each party’s vote total by odd numbers starting from 1.
      • This creates a series of quotients for every party.

      Example: 5,167,429 ÷ 1 = 5,167,429, then ÷ 3 = 1,722,476, then ÷ 5 = 1,033,486.

      Step 2: Award (Assign Seats)

      • Compare the quotients from all eligible parties.
      • Identify the highest active quotient.
      • The party with the highest quotient wins the next seat.

      Seat 1 goes to the highest quotient, seat 2 to the next highest, and so on until all 110 seats are assigned.

      Step 3: Compress (Update the Divisor)

      • After a party wins, move its divisor to the next odd number.
      • Its next quotient becomes smaller.
      • This gives other parties a chance in the following round.

      After winning at ÷ 1, the next quotient is calculated at ÷ 3.
